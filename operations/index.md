Build things that are functional, durable, repairable, and honestly yours.

### Tax Situation

- Day job salary: ~100,000 CHF/year
- Single, no kids, Canton Zürich
- GmbH losses stay in the company (carryforward), don't offset personal income
- Pay yourself via salary (deductible for GmbH) and/or dividends (reduced rate)
- VAT: not registered (revenue far below 100k threshold)

### Liability

**Protects:** business liabilities limited to company assets, ring-fences product sales risk.
**Does not protect:** personal wrongful acts, personal guarantees, director duty failures.

**Creator-specific risks:** copyright/IP, defamation, product liability. Get business liability insurance.

## Research

Use projects as genuine academic research. Publish findings openly. Work towards a PhD by portfolio.

### Pipeline
1. **Blog** — open lab notebook with raw data, methodology, iterations
2. **GitHub** — all data, CAD files, code, schematics under open licenses
3. **Preprint** (arXiv, OSF, Zenodo) — formal write-ups with DOI
4. **Journal** — polished submissions to peer-reviewed journals

### PhD by Portfolio
Submit a collection of published works + ~15,000-word commentary. Takes ~1 year if the body of work exists.
- https://www.southwales.ac.uk/gradschool/postgraduate-research-degrees/phd-by-portfolio/
- https://ssbr-edu.ch/what-exactly-is-a-phd-by-portfolio/
- https://www.kingston.ac.uk/research/research-degrees/available-degrees/practice-based-phd/

### Publishing channels
- Citizen Science: Theory and Practice — https://theoryandpractice.citizenscienceassociation.org/
- JOSS (Journal of Open Source Software) — https://joss.theoj.org
- PLoS ONE — https://blogs.plos.org/citizensci/
- ScienceOpen — https://www.scienceopen.com/

### Platforms
- PubPub (MIT-backed, open-source) — https://www.pubpub.org/
- Zenodo (CERN-backed, free DOIs) — https://zenodo.org/
- OSF (Open Science Framework) — https://osf.io/

### Making it research-grade
- Clear research question (the "Can I...?" format works)
- Reproducible methodology
- Published data (measurements, test results, comparisons)
- Proper citations
- Honest limitations
- Open licenses (CC-BY for text, CERN-OHL for hardware, MIT for software)
