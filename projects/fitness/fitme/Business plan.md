# Business idea
Lift-Pal is a state of the art fitness application that allows athletes and especially coaches to create, amend and distribute their personalised workout plans. Based on the in-built data tracking, coaches can see the live progress and stats of their athletes. Further, the athletes always have access to the latest workout plan of their coach and can see and enter their training progress instantly. 
As Lift-Pal’s integrated AI can analyse the generated workout data on the fly, Lift-Pal actively suggests additional workouts, as recommended exercises, exercise loads or schedules. This allows coaches to offer more customizable workout plans and athletes to actively progress without having to use their own calculators or “bro”-science articles on the internet. 
## Business case in a nut-shell
1. A coach subscribes to lift-pal to become a “coach”-user
2. Coach creates one or more workout plans
3. Each workout plan has a unique access code that can be changed by the coach if needed
4. The athlete(s) pay the coach directly to get a personalised or a standard workout plan (lift-pal does not care)
5. After payment from athlete is processed, the coach gives the respective workout code to the athlete
6. The athlete can then uses the code to create the first login [^1] and access all workout plans for which it has a code
7. Coach can always track the progress if its athletes, adjust and create workout plans

## Porter’s “five” forces
**Threat of new entrants**
This threat seems to be very real given the number of existing fitness apps.

**Bargaining power of users**
The power of users on the price can be very high. Since smartphone users are often not willing to pay anything for a mobile app unless it is very very useful. On the other side, one can see that good fitness apps are not free of charge and still have a significant number of users. Still, there is always the caveat that users will move to a cheaper of free fitness application if a new competitors shows up. 

**Threat of substitute products**
There does not seem to be a substitute for fitness tracking apps. Except a physical notebook or an excel sheet that is also some kind of an application. 

**Bargaining power of suppliers**
For a fitness application there are not typical suppliers. However, a fitness application that is built with own software developers depends on prices for hosting services and other applications that are used for maintaining and storing the code- and database. These services often come with a subscription for licences that can be increased at any time, which is a risk.

**Rivalry between competitors**
The better and cheaper the app, the more users will use it.
